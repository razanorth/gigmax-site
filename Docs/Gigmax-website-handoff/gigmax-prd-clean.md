# GigMax Website PRD v1 (Clean Markdown)

## 1. Project Summary
Build a premium, high-trust marketing website for GigMax, a productized service that helps consumer app companies launch organic UGC programs in-house.

GigMax is not a traditional agency and not a course. The website must make that distinction obvious.

The site should communicate that GigMax:
- builds the operating system for an in-house organic UGC program
- sources and vets creators
- develops creative formats and playbooks
- sets up tracking, payments, and operational infrastructure
- hands the system off to the client so they own it

The website should feel like a real company website, not a pitch deck, not a portfolio, and not a creator personal brand page.

## 2. Primary Goal
Get qualified founders, growth leads, and marketing operators at consumer apps to book a call.

## 3. Secondary Goals
- make the offer understandable within 5 seconds
- establish trust through case studies and testimonials
- explain why GigMax is a better fit than an agency retainer or building from scratch internally
- make the handoff / ownership model feel valuable and clear

## 4. Target Audience
### Primary audience
- founders of consumer apps
- heads of growth
- performance marketers / lifecycle / growth operators
- startup teams who believe organic content matters but do not have a working UGC machine internally

### Audience traits
- they already believe content matters
- they are resource-constrained
- they want speed
- they are skeptical of fluff and vague agencies
- they want a repeatable system, not just random creators
- they care about performance, ownership, speed to launch, and operational simplicity

## 5. Core Positioning
### One-line positioning
GigMax builds and launches your in-house organic UGC program in 14 days for a flat fee. No retainers.

Alternative hero line to evaluate:
We Build It, You Own It.

### Expanded positioning
GigMax helps consumer apps get an organic UGC engine live in 14 days without paying a long-term agency retainer or spending 3 to 6 months building the system internally.

### What GigMax is not
- not a social media agency retainer
- not influencer marketing management
- not a marketplace
- not a consulting-only engagement
- not creator education

## 6. Messaging Priorities
The site should communicate these ideas in roughly this order:
1. organic UGC is a real growth channel
2. most teams know it matters but fail on execution
3. the hard part is not just finding creators, but finding creators that understand what they are doing and won't ghost
4. the hard part is not finding a viral format, it is building a system with training wheels that gets you to find those formats frequently
5. GigMax builds that system quickly
6. clients own the creators, playbooks, and infrastructure after handoff
7. this is faster and cheaper than most alternatives
8. there is proof that this works

## 7. Key Differentiators
- Ownership: the client owns the system after buildout
- Speed: live by day 15
- Creator sourcing: vetted and reliable creators included
- Creative strategy: multiple format playbooks included
- Infrastructure: payment structure, tracking, accountability, and ops setup included
- Productized clarity: flat-fee buildout, not endless retainer ambiguity

## 8. Website Type
Single-page scrolling marketing website.

Not a slide deck.

## 9. Site Architecture
### Section order
1. Hero
2. Social proof / trust bar with transparent logos from everyone listed in the deck on slide 2
3. Problem
4. Why GigMax / value proposition
5. How it works
6. Comparison section
7. Case studies
8. Founder credibility
9. Pricing / fit
10. FAQ
11. Final CTA

## 10. Section-by-Section Requirements

### 10.1 Hero
**Goal:** Make the offer clear immediately.

Must communicate:
- who it is for
- what it does
- why it matters
- primary CTA

Recommended content:
- headline: clear and concrete, not clever
- subheadline: explain the buildout + handoff model
- CTA: Book a call
- secondary CTA: View case studies
- optional small proof line under CTA

Example direction:
Build your in-house organic UGC program in 15 days — creators, playbooks, tracking, and handoff included.

Design notes:
- premium and restrained
- high visual confidence
- no heavy animation in hero
- use spacing and typography to do the work

### 10.2 Social Proof / Trust Bar
**Goal:** reduce skepticism quickly

Content ideas:
- brands worked with: CashApp, Headspace, CourseHero, SuperCoffee, a0.dev, Zencastr, Ivory Mind
- optional proof metrics in small cards

Design notes:
- should feel subtle and premium
- not a noisy logo wall

### 10.3 Problem
**Goal:** show deep understanding of why teams fail

Core problems to communicate:
- creators ghost
- ops become messy and time-consuming
- internal launch takes too long
- agencies are expensive and leave you dependent

Format:
- 3 to 4 clean cards
- short, sharp copy
- no giant paragraphs

### 10.4 Why GigMax / Value Prop
**Goal:** explain the actual deliverable

Must communicate that clients get:
- 5 to 10 vetted creators
- 12 content formats / creative playbooks
- custom payment structure
- tracking and accountability system
- ops setup and handoff training

Format options:
- checklist grid
- split section with copy on left and proof visual on right

### 10.5 How It Works
**Goal:** make the process feel simple, fast, and believable

Steps:
- kickoff
- creator sourcing and vetting
- creative playbooks
- contracts / payments / tracking setup
- handoff and training

Design notes:
- avoid overdesigned timeline gimmicks
- should feel operational and clean
- use one supporting product/dashboard visual

### 10.6 Comparison Section
**Goal:** make the buying decision easier

Compare GigMax against:
- agency retainer
- hiring / building internally
- optional UGC Engineer category if useful

Compare on:
- cost
- speed to launch
- ownership
- creator sourcing
- creative strategy
- what happens after engagement ends

Important:
This section must feel fair and factual, not angry or salesy.

### 10.7 Case Studies
**Goal:** prove the model with real outcomes

#### Case Study A: a0.dev
Use the strongest performance proof. Potential proof points:
- 8.6M views
- 810K likes
- 9.5% engagement
- peak video 757K
- sign-ups moved from roughly 20/day to 130+/day
- 866 posts from 430 unique videos

#### Case Study B: Ivory Mind
Use as proof of speed + reliability + testimonial quality. Potential proof points:
- 10-day creator delivery
- first viral video within 3 weeks
- Rajya testimonial

#### Case Study C: Zencastr
Use as proof of scale and international applicability. Potential proof points:
- 1.4M views
- 355 posts
- 150 unique videos
- $0.70 CPM
- 3.9% engagement
- 24.2K+ interactions

Case study format requirements:
- each case study should be skimmable
- lead with business-relevant proof
- include screenshot or proof visual where helpful
- do not overload the page with too many images at once

### 10.8 Founder Credibility
**Goal:** make Raza feel credible without making the site about him

Content direction:
- brief founder section
- mention 1M+ followers, 1B+ views, 11 years in creator economy
- show relevant brand experience
- frame this as why the operator understands content systems

Important:
This should support the company, not turn the site into a creator portfolio.

### 10.9 Pricing / Fit
**Goal:** qualify leads and make the offer feel real

Pricing direction:
GigMax Buildout  
$15K–$20K flat, based on scope  
one-time

Include:
- what is included
- who it is best for
- who it is not for

Best-for examples:
- consumer apps
- teams that want to own the program internally
- teams that value speed and operational clarity

Not-for examples:
- brands looking for a cheap marketplace solution
- teams that want a forever-agency retainer
- teams that are not ready to manage creators after handoff

### 10.10 FAQ
**Goal:** handle objections cleanly

Suggested questions:
- What types of companies is GigMax best for?
- Do you manage the program forever?
- What happens after handoff?
- Are creators included?
- How is this different from an agency?
- How fast can we launch?
- What does the flat fee depend on?

### 10.11 Final CTA
**Goal:** convert

Requirements:
- strong headline
- one clear CTA
- repeat trust / clarity in one line
- easy contact option

## 11. Design Direction
### Brand feeling
- premium
- editorial
- modern
- minimal
- credible
- strategic
- sharp

### Should feel like
- a high-end B2B service brand
- a company that understands both internet culture and operations

### Should not feel like
- a startup pitch deck
- a keynote presentation
- a generic SaaS landing page
- a creator portfolio
- a trendy agency site full of empty motion

### Visual direction
- clean layout
- strong typography
- restrained palette
- calm whitespace
- subtle interactions
- high information hierarchy

### Color guidance
Current beige / cream direction can work, but it should be tightened. Use it in a more web-native way. The final site should feel more like a premium editorial service site than a full-screen slide deck.

## 12. Content and Asset Inputs
Use these real assets from the current materials where appropriate:
- founder photo
- Rajya testimonial screenshot
- a0.dev proof screenshots
- dashboard / tracking screenshot
- case study metrics already used in current materials

Do not fabricate metrics. Do not invent new logos, proof points, or testimonials.

## 13. Functional Requirements
- responsive across mobile, tablet, laptop, desktop
- built as a scrolling website, not slide navigation
- CTA visible in hero and repeated throughout page
- fast load time
- accessible typography and contrast
- clean hover states
- optional lightbox/modal for proof images
- no reliance on external CMS required for v1
- easy to edit copy in code

## 14. Technical Requirements
### Preferred stack
- React
- Tailwind CSS
- simple component structure
- clean reusable sections

### Implementation guidance
- avoid overengineering
- no complex state unless needed
- no heavy animation libraries unless clearly beneficial
- motion should be subtle and secondary

### Suggested file structure
- page.tsx or App.tsx
- components/sections/Hero.tsx
- components/sections/ProofBar.tsx
- components/sections/Problem.tsx
- components/sections/WhyGigMax.tsx
- components/sections/HowItWorks.tsx
- components/sections/Comparison.tsx
- components/sections/CaseStudies.tsx
- components/sections/Founder.tsx
- components/sections/Pricing.tsx
- components/sections/FAQ.tsx
- components/sections/CTA.tsx
- lib/content.ts

## 15. UX Requirements
- user should understand the offer within 5 seconds
- user should understand the difference between GigMax and an agency within 20 seconds
- case studies should be easy to skim
- page should feel trustworthy before it feels fancy
- mobile version should preserve credibility and clarity
- layout should encourage downward scrolling naturally

## 16. Non-Goals
Do not build:
- slide-based navigation
- full-screen presentation mechanics
- autoplay carousels
- excessive parallax
- overly dark, flashy “agency” aesthetics
- generic AI-generated section filler
- long dense founder-story writing
- multiple pages unless requested later

## 17. Acceptance Criteria
The project is successful if:
- the hero clearly states the offer and target customer
- the site feels like a real B2B service website
- the problem and solution are obvious without reading every word
- GigMax is clearly differentiated from agencies and internal build-from-scratch
- all proof points used are real and legible
- the page looks strong on mobile
- CTA appears at least 3 times
- no part of the site feels like a presentation deck
- the site is polished enough to send to prospects immediately

## 18. Build Sequence for Claude
### Phase 1: Wireframe
Build the full one-page site layout with real section structure and placeholder styling. No fancy animations. No speculative copy.

### Phase 2: Real content integration
Insert the real GigMax copy, assets, proof points, and case study content.

### Phase 3: Visual polish
Improve spacing, typography, card design, proof hierarchy, and CTA treatment.

### Phase 4: QA
Check mobile, tablet, and desktop. Fix overflow, spacing, inconsistent section rhythm, and proof readability.
